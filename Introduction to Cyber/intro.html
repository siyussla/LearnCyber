Introduction to Cybersecurity

Basics of Cybersecurity Concepts

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, and damage. In an increasingly interconnected world, cybersecurity is crucial to safeguard sensitive information and ensure operational continuity.

Key concepts:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to view it.
  • Integrity: Protecting data from being altered by unauthorized parties.
  • Availability: Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed.
  • Authentication: Verifying the identity of users or devices.
  • Authorization: Granting permissions to authenticated entities to access specific resources.

Importance of Web Application Security

Web applications are a prime target for cyberattacks because of their accessibility and role in storing sensitive data. Common threats include:

  • SQL Injection (SQLi): Attackers manipulate a database by injecting malicious queries.
  • Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Malicious scripts are injected into web pages, compromising users' data.
  • Broken Authentication: Poor authentication mechanisms that allow attackers to gain unauthorized access.

Securing web applications ensures the safety of sensitive information, prevents financial losses, and maintains the trust of users.
